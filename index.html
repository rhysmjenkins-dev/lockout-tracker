📚 GLOSSARY OF TERMS

Game rules and app features explained

🎴 Game Terms

Lockout

Declaring that you have 5 points or less at the start of your turn. If successful (strictly lowest score), you score 0 for the hand.

False Lockout

A failed lockout attempt where either your score is greater than 5, or you're tied/higher than another player. Results in your lockout score + penalty.

Lockout Score

The actual hand total you had when you declared lockout (e.g., 3 points). Recorded separately for statistics even if you score 0.

Hand

A single round of play, from deal to lockout reveal. Multiple hands make up a session.

Session

A complete game consisting of multiple hands. Ends when players decide (fixed hands, score target, or event end).

Formation

The arrangement of your face-down cards (typically 2×2 or 1×4). Cards must stay in their original positions for memory tracking.

Magic Cards

Cards with special abilities when drawn from the deck: 7 (look at own card), 8 (look at opponent's card), 9 (blind swap), 10 (open swap), Red Jack (swap between two opponents).

Slam

An out-of-turn action where you match a discarded card with one in your hand or an opponent's hand. Reduces hand size or penalizes opponents.

Penalty Card

A face-down card added to your hand (without looking) as punishment for incorrect slams or being correctly slammed by an opponent.

Defense

Protecting your card from being slammed by placing your finger on it first when you see an opponent approaching.

Late Joiner

A player who joins mid-session. Their starting score equals the average of all current players' totals at the hand they join.

Cumulative Score

Your total score across all hands in a session. Lowest cumulative score wins.

Red King

The only card worth -1 points (King of Hearts or Diamonds). Cannot be slammed.

📱 App-Specific Terms

Active Session

A session currently in progress (not yet ended). Can be resumed from the home screen.

Previous Sessions

Completed sessions that have been ended. Viewable with full stats and graphs.

Worm Chart (Cricket Worm)

A line graph showing cumulative scores over time for all players in a session.

Manhattan Chart

A bar chart showing hand-by-hand scores for all players in a session.

Head-to-Head Record

Direct win/loss record between two players when they compete in the same session (who finished with lower score).

Sessions Together

Statistics for two players when they both participated in the same session.

All Sessions

Individual statistics for a player across every session they've played (regardless of opponents).

Performance Context

How a player's head-to-head record against another player varies depending on who else is playing.

Lockout Rate

Percentage of hands where a player successfully locked out (successful lockouts ÷ total hands played).

False Lockout Rate

Percentage of lockout attempts that were false (false lockouts ÷ total lockout attempts).

Average Lockout Score

The mean score of all successful lockouts by a player. Lower is better (indicates locking out with minimal points).

Average Hand

Mean score per hand played (total score ÷ hands played). Excludes starting scores for late joiners.

Hand Streak

Consecutive hands won (successful lockouts). Resets on false lockout or non-lockout hand.

Win Rate

Percentage of sessions won (sessions won ÷ sessions played).

Session Tags

Categories applied to sessions (e.g., Casual, Holiday, Pub, Tournament) for filtering and organization.

Edit History

A log of all changes made to sessions and hands, tracking who edited what and when.

ELO Rating

A number representing a player's skill level. All players start at 1000. Goes up when you finish higher than expected, down when you finish lower. Visible on the home screen and stats page.

Provisional Rating (?)

A rating marked with ? meaning the player has played fewer than 50 hands. Provisional ratings move faster to help find your true level quickly. Once you hit 50 hands the ? disappears and your rating stabilises.

K-Factor

Controls how much your ELO changes per session. Provisional players use K=40 (bigger swings), established players use K=24 (more stable). Named after the chess rating system it's borrowed from.

Rank-Based ELO

ELO calculated from finishing position (1st, 2nd, 3rd etc.) rather than head-to-head results. Handles any number of players fairly — total rating change always sums to zero across all players in a session.

📱 How to Use the Lockout Tracker

📊 OVERVIEW

This app tracks your Lockout game sessions, records hand-by-hand scores, and generates detailed statistics. Players only input lockout scores and the app handles all calculations automatically.

🚀 GETTING STARTED

1. Add Players (One-Time Setup)

  • Tap "➕ Add New Player" from the home screen
  • Enter player name and tap "Add Player"
  • Repeat for all regular players
  • Players only need to be added once (they're saved permanently)

2. Start a New Session

  • Tap "🎮 Start New Session" from the home screen
  • Fill in:
    • Session Title (e.g., "Magaluf 2024", "Friday Night")
    • Host (who's organizing/tracking)
    • Session Notes (optional - e.g., "Playing at John's house")
    • Session Type (optional tags - Casual, Holiday, Pub, etc.)
    • False Lockout Penalty (default: 10 points)
    • Select Players (check all players participating)
  • Tap "Start Session"

🎮 DURING A SESSION

Recording Hands

For each hand:

  1. Enter Lockout Scores
    • Input the lockout score for each player (the score they called lockout with)
    • Example: If a player locked out with 3 points, enter 3
  2. Select Who Locked Out
    • Tap the radio button next to the player who declared lockout
  3. False Lockout Detection
    • The app automatically checks if the lockout is valid:
      • ✅ Valid: Score ≤ 5 AND strictly lowest
      • ❌ False: Score > 5 OR tied/higher than another player
    • A warning appears if it's a false lockout
    • Confirm to proceed (penalty is applied automatically)
  4. Add Hand Note (Optional)
    • Add comments like "Epic comeback" or "Close call"
  5. Tap "Submit Hand"
    • Scores are calculated automatically:
      • Successful lockout: Player scores 0 (or keeps negative score)
      • False lockout: Player scores lockout score + penalty
      • Other players: Keep their lockout scores

What the App Calculates

The app automatically handles:

  • ✅ Successful lockout → 0 points (negative scores preserved)
  • ✅ False lockout → lockout score + penalty
  • ✅ Cumulative session scores
  • ✅ Hand-by-hand history
  • ✅ Live statistics (avg hand, lockout rate, etc.)
  • ✅ Real-time graphs (Worm chart, Manhattan chart)

Adding a Late Joiner

If a player joins mid-session:

  1. Tap "➕ Add Player" button (in active session)
  2. Select the player from the list
  3. Confirm — they'll join from the current hand onwards
  4. The app calculates their starting score automatically (average of all current players' totals)
  5. Late joiners are marked with a "Joined H#" badge

Editing/Deleting Hands

  • Edit a hand: Tap "Edit" next to any hand in the history
    • Modify scores, change who locked out, update notes
    • False lockout detection runs again
  • Delete the most recent hand: Tap "Delete" (only available for the last hand)

Editing Session Details

  • Tap "✏️ Edit Session" to update:
    • Session notes
    • Session tags/type

Viewing Live Stats

During an active session, scroll down to see:

  • Current Scores Table (total, avg hand, lockout rate, etc.)
  • Session Statistics (total hands, avg score/hand, leader, biggest gap)
  • Lockout Performance (who has the best avg lockout score)
  • Graphs (Worm chart shows cumulative scores, Manhattan shows hand-by-hand)

Ending a Session

  • Tap "End Session" when finished
  • Session is marked as complete and moves to Previous Sessions

📊 VIEWING PAST SESSIONS

Browse Previous Sessions

  • Tap "📊 View Previous Sessions" from home screen
  • Tap any session to view full details:
    • Final scores table
    • Hand-by-hand breakdown
    • Graphs (Worm, Manhattan)
    • Session notes/tags

📈 STATISTICS & COMPARISONS

Overall Stats

  • Tap "📈 View Stats" → "Overall Stats"
  • See:
    • Total sessions/hands played
    • Most sessions/hands won
    • Best win rates (session & hand level)
    • Lowest avg score/hand
    • Best avg lockout score
    • Longest hand streak
    • Most false lockouts
    • Player Breakdown Table (all stats per player)

Head-to-Head Records

  • Tap "📈 View Stats" → "Head-to-Head Records"
  • See direct records when two players compete in the same session
  • Shows win/loss records (who finished with lower score)
  • Tap "View Detailed Comparison" for any matchup

Detailed Player Comparison

  • Tap "📈 View Stats" → "Detailed Comparison"

  • See:
    • Sessions Together: Head-to-head record when both played
    • All Sessions: Individual performance across all sessions

    • Session History: Clickable list of all sessions they played together

❓ TROUBLESHOOTING

Q: Can I edit a hand after submitting?

A: Yes! Tap "Edit" next to any hand in the history. Only the most recent hand can be deleted.

Q: What if I accidentally end a session?

A: Ended sessions cannot be reopened. Start a new session if needed.

Q: Can I remove a player from a session?

A: No, once a player is added (either at start or mid-session), they cannot be removed. Plan carefully!

Q: What if two players tie for lowest after a lockout?

A: The locked-out player gets a false lockout penalty. The other tied player keeps their original score (not reset to 0).

Q: How are late joiner starting scores calculated?

A: Average of all current players' cumulative totals at the hand they join.

Q: Why does the app ask for lockout scores, not final scores?

A: The app calculates final scores automatically based on lockout validity. You only input the score the player called lockout with.

⚡ ELO RATINGS

What is ELO?

  • A skill rating system borrowed from chess — see ELO Rating in glossary
  • All players start at 1000
  • Goes up when you finish higher than expected
  • Goes down when you finish lower than expected
  • Total rating change always sums to zero across all players in a session — see Rank-Based ELO
  • Testing sessions are excluded from ELO calculations

Provisional Ratings (?)

  • New players show a ? next to their rating (e.g. 1045?)
  • Provisional = under 50 hands played
  • Provisional ratings move faster (K=40) to find your true level quickly — what is K-Factor?
  • Once you hit 50 hands the ? disappears and rating stabilises (K=24)
  • The 50-hand threshold is checked at the start of each session — K won't switch mid-session

Where to Find ELO

  • Home screen — collapsible ELO leaderboard widget (tap ⚡ ELO Rankings to expand)
  • Active session — rating badge shown next to each player's name
  • Stats → ⚡ ELO Rankings — full table and rating history graph

Reading the Rating History Graph

  • Each line represents one player's rating over time
  • X-axis = sessions in date order
  • If a player missed a session their line stays flat for that session

📜 Lockout - Official Rules

🎯 OBJECTIVE

Be the player with the lowest cumulative score at the end of the session by winning hands through successful lockouts.

🎴 SETUP

Players: 2 or more

Deck: Standard 52-card deck

Deal:

  1. Choose a dealer (rotates clockwise each hand)
  2. Deal 4 cards face down to each player
  3. Players arrange their 4 cards in a formation in front of them (common: 2×2 grid or 1×4 row)
  4. Each player may secretly look at any 2 of their 4 cards, then place them back face down
  5. Place remaining deck face down in the center (draw pile)
  6. Player to the dealer's left goes first
⚠️ Important: Cards must stay in their original positions throughout the hand. Players rely on memory to track card locations.

💎 CARD VALUES

Card Value
Ace 1
2-10 Face value
Jack, Queen, King 10
Red King ♥️ ♦️ -1

Your score = sum of all cards in your hand

🔄 TURN SEQUENCE

On your turn:

1. DRAW one card from either:

  • The face-down draw pile, OR
  • The top card of the face-up discard pile

2. CHOOSE one of the following:

  • Swap the drawn card with one of your face-down cards (place the old card face up on discard pile)
  • Discard the drawn card face up onto the discard pile (keep your hand unchanged)
ℹ️ First Turn Exception: The first player must draw from the draw pile (no discard pile exists yet).
ℹ️ When Draw Pile Runs Out: Shuffle the discard pile face down to create a new draw pile, leaving only the current top card as the discard pile.

✨ MAGIC CARDS

When you draw these cards from the draw pile (not discard), you may use their special ability before deciding to swap or discard:

Card Ability
7 Look at one of your own face-down cards
8 Look at one of another player's face-down cards
9 Blind Swap - Swap one of your cards with another player's card (neither card is looked at)
10 Open Swap - Pick up one of your cards and one of another player's cards, look at both, then either swap them or return them to their original positions
Red Jack
(Optional)		 Swap one card between any two other players (not yourself). Cards remain face down and stay in their original positions
⚠️ Note: Cards must stay in their original table positions after swaps. Magic abilities only work when drawn from the draw pile, not when picked from the discard pile.

👊 SLAMS

⚡ Slams can happen at any time, out of turn!

When a player discards a card (e.g., a 5), you may slam if you believe a matching card exists:

Slamming Your Own Card

  1. Quickly turn over the matching card from your hand
  2. If correct: Place it on the discard pile (you now have one fewer card = lower score!)
  3. If incorrect: Take a 1-card penalty card from the draw pile (place face down in your hand without looking)

Slamming Another Player's Card

  1. Quickly place your finger on their card
  2. Turn it over to reveal
  3. If correct: They take a 1-card penalty card from the draw pile (placed face down without looking)
  4. If incorrect: You take a 1-card penalty card

Slam Rules

  • Suits don't matter (only card values)
  • Red Kings cannot be slammed
  • You cannot slam on a card you just discarded — BUT if another player slams before the next discard, the top card becomes "fresh" and you may slam
  • Once the next player discards, the card below is no longer slammable
  • Multiple players can slam simultaneously (4 of each card type exist in the deck)
  • Defense: You may protect your card by placing your finger on it first when you see someone approaching to slam
ℹ️ Penalty Cards: Drawn from the draw pile, placed face down in your hand without looking. Added to your formation (formations can grow beyond 4 cards). Cannot be rearranged or moved (unless removed via slam).

🔒 LOCKOUT

Lockout is how you win a hand!

When to Lockout

  • Only at the start of your turn (before drawing)
  • Your hand total must be 5 or less (minimum: -2 with two Red Kings)
  • Only one player can lockout per hand

What Happens After Lockout

  1. You announce "Lockout!" (do not reveal your hand yet)
  2. Your hand is frozen (no swaps, slams, or other interference)
  3. Play continues clockwise — every other player gets one final turn
  4. When play returns to you, all players reveal their cards simultaneously

✅ Successful Lockout

You score 0 for the hand if:

  • Your hand total is 5 or less, AND
  • Your total is strictly lower than all other players (no ties!)

Exception: If your total is negative, you keep the negative score (it's a benefit!)

❌ False Lockout

You score your hand total + penalty if:

  • Your hand total is greater than 5, OR
  • Your total is tied with or higher than another player

Penalty amount is decided at the start of the session (typically 10 points).

Other Players

All other players score their final hand totals (no change to 0, even if they have the lowest score after a false lockout).

🏆 WINNING THE GAME

  • Lowest cumulative score across all hands wins the session
  • Sessions end when players decide (fixed number of hands, score target, end of event, etc.)

📋 ADDITIONAL RULES

Communication

  • Bluffing and conferring about cards (truthful or not) is allowed
  • Share information strategically to influence other players' decisions

Memory is Key

  • Cards never move positions (except when removed via slam)
  • Track what you've seen through magic cards and discards
  • Remember what other players know about your hand

💡 STRATEGY TIPS

  • ✅ Use magic cards wisely — knowing card locations is powerful
  • ✅ Slam aggressively — reducing your hand size lowers your score
  • ✅ Defend your cards — be ready to protect when you see opponents approaching
  • ✅ Bluff strategically — mislead opponents about what you know
  • ✅ Time your lockout — ensure you're strictly lowest before declaring
Good luck, and may the lowest score win! 🎴
